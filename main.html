The Collapse of the Soviet Union

On December 25, 1991, the Soviet hammer and sickle flag lowered for the last time over the Kremlin, thereafter replaced by the Russian flag. It signals the end of an era of the Cold War and the USSR. Also, it exhibited that extreme or complete communism isn't a feasible ideology. Ironically, on the eve of the collapse, a large-scale survey of views within the CPSU(Communist Party of the Soviet Union) argued that the party had a great future since its members had adapted to 'general civilisational values of freedom of conscienc, civil society, and the market economy'.

Since 1988 the USSR gradually lost control of Moscow, Baltic republics, Caucasus, and Central Asian Republics, etc. And in the 1990 Estonian Supreme Soviet election, USSR decided to give up the monopoly of political control. In the following elections, USSR eventually lost its dominance in 6 Republics among its 15 Republics.

On August 24, 1991, Gorbachev resigned as the general secretary of the Central Committee of the CPSU(Communist Party of the Soviet Union).and dissolved all other parties in the government. Five days after, the Supreme Soviet suspended all the CPSU activities on Soviet Territory, which effectively ended the communist's rule in USSR.

The Soviet Union collapsed with dramatic speed in the last quarter of 1991. Among the rest of the 10 constitutional Republics, Ukraine first decided to seceded. By the end of September, Gorbachev was no longer able to influence the affairs out of Moscow. And in the November 7, 1991, most newspapers referred this country as the 'former Soviet Union'.

In 1991 December 1, Ukraine, the second largest country in USSR, had an popular referendum. The referendum showed that 91% of voters prefered independency which confirmed that Ukraine will officially secede from USSR. The disintegration seems to be unchangeable despite the efforts that Gorbachev had made. In a nationally televised speech in the evening of December 25, 1991, Gorbachev resigned as president of the USSR. And he declared the extinct of USSR office.

On the night of December 25, at 7:32 p.m., after Gorbachev left the Kremlin. The Soviet flag was lowered and the State Anthem of the Soviet Union was played for the last time. The Russian flag raise afterward.

The following day, Yeltsin moved into Gorbachev's former office. And the rest of few institutions left ceased operation. And the individual republics that split after the Soviet disintegrated would decide their central government's role.
